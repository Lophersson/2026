Our Philosophy

Travel as a
state of mind

We do not sell destinations.
We orchestrate transformations.

Mavara Privé was born from a conviction that true luxury in travel is no longer about thread counts or Michelin stars. It is about encountering the world with a depth of access that only trust, patience, and genuine knowledge can provide.

We work with a deliberately small number of families and individuals each year. Not because exclusivity is a marketing device — but because intimacy is the only way to do this properly. Every journey is singular. Built once, lived once.

Our reach spans the temples of Angkor and the rice terraces of Bali, the outer atolls of the Maldives and the lesser-known islands of the Seychelles — always guided by the same principle: take you somewhere real.

I
Intimacy
A maximum of 24 journeys crafted per year. Your advisor becomes your companion.
II
Depth
Months of preparation for every trip. Cultural immersion, not cultural tourism.
III
Silence
No crowds, no queues. Access to places that do not appear on any map we publish.

How we work

From first conversation
to last memory

The Conversation
We listen first
A private consultation — in person in Lisbon or Barcelona, or by secure video — where we understand not just where you want to go, but who you are when you travel. No forms, no questionnaires.
The Design
We build your world
Three to four weeks of silent work. We contact our network of private guides, property owners and local custodians. We present one proposal, precisely calibrated. Not options — a vision.
The Refinement
We perfect together
Two rounds of dialogue to adjust rhythm, depth, and pace. We do not rush. A journey planned over months is a journey felt differently once you arrive.
The Journey
We travel with you
Your advisor remains available at every hour. If something extraordinary becomes possible — a private temple access, a master artisan encounter — we open that door. Always.

What we curate

Moments that
do not repeat

Private Access
Temples and heritage sites opened exclusively for your group. Angkor at 4 AM, the Shwedagon terrace before dawn, the Tanah Lot clifftop at dusk without a single stranger present.
Cultural & Spiritual
Untouched Seas
Private sailing through the Banda Sea. A chartered phinisi with a marine biologist aboard, navigating reefs that receive fewer than a hundred visitors a year. The ocean as it was.
Nautical & Marine
The Living Table
A private dinner cooked by a third-generation Balinese chef in her family compound. A Thai royal court menu prepared in a teak palace. Cuisine as cultural inheritance, not performance.
Gastronomic & Cultural
Master Artisans
A private session with a Khmer shadow-puppet maker in Siem Reap. An afternoon with a Sumatran batik master. Learning the language of a culture through its makers, not its markets.
Arts & Craftsmanship
Wellness Sanctuaries
A private retreat in the highlands of Chiang Mai. Ayurvedic programmes in a Keralan estate closed to the public. Silence as a luxury — deeply, genuinely sought.
Wellness & Stillness
Children & Legacy
Journeys designed for families who believe travel is the finest education. A private wildlife field station in Borneo. A two-day river expedition in the heart of Sarawak with a naturalist guide.
